Aggregations & Grouping

One franchise built an absolute runs fortress in 2019

While Chennai maintained historical consistency, franchises like Mumbai Indians and Kings XI Punjab executed a high-volume runs strategy that reset the tournament baseline.

SELECT Team, SUM(Runs) AS Total_Run
FROM `ipl_analysis.2019_batsman`
GROUP BY Team
ORDER BY Total_Run DESC;
Complex Conditional Filtering

Bowlers who dominated under high-pressure conditions

Match outcomes are won in defensive phases. In 2018, Chennai Super Kings utilized unique 4-wicket game-changing spells to secure their defensive advantages.

SELECT Player, Team, _4w
FROM `ipl_analysis.2018_bowlers`
WHERE _4w > 0
ORDER BY _4w DESC;

Relational Joins

True multi-utility players are rare

Out of hundreds of players, only a handful performed at elite levels in both disciplines. SQL's INNER JOIN identifies these statistical anomalies like A. Russell.

SELECT p.Player, p.Team
FROM `ipl_analysis.2019_batsmen` AS p 
INNER JOIN `ipl_analysis.2019_bowlers` AS a 
ON p.Player = a.Player;

Multi-table Self-Joins

Extreme consistency across 24 months

Player performance fluctuates wildly between seasons. However, linking multi-year datasets reveals the elite few who maintained extreme high-performance standards.

SELECT n.Player, e.Runs AS Runs_2018, n.Runs AS Runs_2019
FROM `ipl_analysis.2018_batsman_new` AS e 
INNER JOIN `ipl_analysis.2019_batsman` AS n 
ON e.Player = n.Player
ORDER BY n.Runs DESC LIMIT 5;
Derived Custom Metrics

Boundary reliance: Clean-hitting efficiency

It isn't just about total runs; it's about speed. Creating calculated columns dynamically evaluates player scoring mechanisms to find the most explosive hitters.

SELECT Player, 
(fours * 4 + sixes * 6) AS boundary_runs
FROM `ipl_analysis.2018_batsman_new`
ORDER BY boundary_runs DESC LIMIT 5;
